The Problem

A loop with
no exit.

Frontline workers move daily between three high-risk environments with no safe step in between. The result is chronic hypervigilance — and a fear that follows them home.

"There were nights I'd come home and just stand at the door, not wanting to touch anything."
— ICU Nurse, COVID unit
"I just wanted a clear routine that told me it was safe."
— Family partner
1
🏥

High-risk hospital shift

12-hour shifts in active-exposure units. Full PPE required. Dominant infection risk.

2
🚇

Crowded MTA commute

Packed subway cars with no isolation. Where psychological load accumulates.

3
🏠

Small shared apartment

Partners, children, roommates. No physical separation. Fear of infecting family is the top concern.

✗
📋

Existing programs failed

Scattered hotel spreadsheets, inconsistent cleaning, and exhausting applications that workers couldn't complete.

The Solution

One application.
One verified match.
One clinical-grade stay.

Mednest replaces administrative chaos with clinical transparency — a mobile-first platform that aggregates hotel, dormitory, and corporate housing into a single worker-facing experience, governed by uniform safety standards.

🔐

Hospital-Verified Identity

A single form pulls credentials from participating hospital HR systems. Workers never re-enter the same information twice.

📍

Proximity-Based Matching

Enter your hospital; Mednest returns the nearest verified safe-stay options within minutes — no manual search, no uneven availability.

🧪

Standardized Clinical Metrics

Every option displays verified attributes: COVID testing cadence, HEPA filtration, daily deep cleaning, UV-C treatment, PPE disposal, and quiet hours.

💬

Community Without Exposure

Optional group chats by building and floor let workers in the same space connect — reducing isolation without physical contact.

🚌

Clean Commute Shuttle

Outer-borough and NJ inventory paired with a dedicated sanitized shuttle synchronized to hospital shift changes.

🏛️

Always-Ready Infrastructure

Block-lease agreements and certifications stay warm between disasters — reactivatable in 72 hours, not months.

Fairness by Design

Who gets in,
and on what terms.

A disaster-response product that serves only the most visible workers repeats the inequity of the disaster itself. Mednest's fairness principles are built into the matching system, not added as policy language.

01

Role Inclusivity

Every credentialed frontline role — nurses, orderlies, EVS, food service — is a first-class user, not an afterthought.

02

Shift-Aware Matching

Night-shift workers get rooms and shuttles that serve their actual schedule, not a daytime default.

03

Geographic Fairness

The Clean Commute shuttle ensures workers from Queens, the Bronx, Staten Island, and NJ have equal access.

04

Language & Accessibility

Available in the dominant languages of the NYC healthcare workforce from launch, not added later.

05

Scarcity Allocation

When demand exceeds supply, allocation uses transparent exposure risk and household vulnerability — never seniority or hospital prestige.

Ecosystem

The network that
makes it work.

Mednest is a coordination layer. The network around it determines whether the product succeeds or collapses under surge.

🏥

Hospitals

HR systems verify credentials. Employee-benefits programs subsidize stays. Infection-control teams co-author clinical standards.

🏨

Housing Supply

Hotel chains provide elastic urban inventory. Universities provide block-leased dorms. Corporate housing handles multi-week surges.

🏛️

Public Sector

NYC Emergency Management activates the network. FEMA and public-health grants reimburse directly so workers never see a bill.

🚌

Logistics & Services

Contracted transit fleets run Clean Commute routes. Food delivery, tele-therapy, and rapid testing are integrated via APIs — not rebuilt in-house.
